What we find

The most common sources of cloud waste

These are the issues we find in almost every audit — and they add up fast.

01
Idle EC2 instances
Servers spun up for a project, demo, or test — and never turned off. Often the single biggest source of waste.
02
Oversized RDS databases
Databases provisioned "just in case" at 4x the size actually needed. Rightsizing alone can save hundreds per month.
03
Dev environments running 24/7
Development and staging environments that run around the clock when they only need to be active during office hours.
04
Unattached EBS volumes
Storage volumes left behind after instances are terminated. Invisible on the bill but quietly accumulating cost.
05
S3 without lifecycle rules
Object storage that grows forever with no expiry policies. Old logs and backups from years ago still being paid for.
06
Unassociated Elastic IPs
Reserved IP addresses not attached to anything but still incurring a charge every single month.
